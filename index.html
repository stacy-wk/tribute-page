Malala Yousafzai

An activist, A Nobel Prize laureate, A feminist.

Here's a brief outline of Malala's life:

  • 1997: Born in the Swat District of Pakistan's northwestern Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province to Ziauddin Yousafzai and Toor Pekai Yousafzai.
  • 2008: Started speaking about education rights when her father took her to Peshawar to speak at the local press club.
  • 2009: Began as a trainee and was then a peer educator in the Institute for War and Peace Reporting's Open Minds Pakistan youth programme.
  • 2011: Received Pakistan's first National Youth Peace Prize.
  • 2012: Shot by a Taliban gunman in an assassination attempt targeting her for her activism.
  • 2013: Co-authored I Am Malala, an international best seller and she received the Sakharov Prize.
  • 2014: Aged 17 at the time, she was the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate.
  • 2015: Became the subject of the Oscar-shortlisted documentary He Named Me Malala.
  • 2017: Was awarded honorary Canadian citizenship and became the youngest person to address the House of Commons of Canada.
  • 2018: Received the Gleitsman Award from the Center for the Public Leadership at Harvard Kennedy School.
  • 2020: Graduated from Oxford with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Philosophy, Politics and Economics (PPE).
  • 2023: Became the youngest ever Honorary Fellow at Linacre College, Oxford.

“When the whole world is silent, even one voice becomes powerful.”

-Malala Yousafzai
